My travel blog

May 2016

Springfield, IL

The Lincoln-themed trip was underway. I landed in St. Louis, grabbed my rental car, and took off for
Springfield, IL. I managed to arrive in time to walk around and snap a couple of pics before it got dark.

The next day, I got up and took a tour of what was basically the only house Abraham Lincoln ever owned.

While I could have stayed there all day, there were baseball games to be seen, so I hopped in the car and made my way to Cincinnati.

Cincinnati, OH

I was running a little bit behind schedule, so I was worried I might not get to the game on time. Fortunately, the driving
rainstorm that was parked over Cincinnati when I arrived was going to delay the game a bit, so I was thankful for that.

I stayed for three games and the Mariners won all three, so that made for a really enjoyable trip. But with the baseball part out of the way, it was time to get back to Lincoln.
I turned the car south and headed for Hodgenville, KY, where Lincoln was born.
